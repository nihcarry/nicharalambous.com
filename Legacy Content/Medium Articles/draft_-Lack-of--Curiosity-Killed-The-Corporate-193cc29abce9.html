The Disease

I recently found out that I suffer from a disease that affects millions of people around the world. The symptoms present the same in all victims but may show up at different stages in life. 

For me the itch started fairly young in life and it’s runs in my family so there was little chance of my escaping it. I know I stand here and look quite healthy but I can promise you that I’m a little bit broken. 

Over time I started to drift towards other people who suffered from the same affliction without realising it. We would meet and have breakfast and coffees while discussing our various manifestations. 

While engaging in one of these regular meetups held at the trendiest spots available I finally realised what I had been suffering from. That’s when the diagnosis was confirmed. 

I suffer from an incurable curiousity that manifests in the never ending need to build things, mostly businesses. 

This disease, the Incurable Curiousity, or IC as it’s more commonly known, affects about 38 million americans right now. Some suffer more than others. The disease can wipe out entire fortunes but it can also turn ideas into billion dollar businesses. 

If you are scared that you or someone you know may have IC, look out for insomnia brought on by a never ending hunger to answer questions. A voracious apetite for knowledge. The inability to let anything go. The inability to see a problem and not try to solve. The inability to shut up, very often leading to the end of relationships. A never ending hunger to seek out the truth, find solutions and to impliment them. 

Notable, long time sufferers include Bill Gates, Steve Jobs, Elon Musk, Patrice Motsepe, Nelson Mandela, Jeff Bezos, Marissa Mayer, Maya Angelou, Oprah and many, many more throughout history. Napolean and Christopher Columbus rank among the most daring recorded in history. 


IC is the gift and the curse of almost every entrepreneur that I’ve ever met. The lack of curiosity has also lead to the downfall of more businesses than I can even count, but we’ll get to that.
