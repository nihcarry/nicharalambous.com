CuriosityAnd Company Culture

There is a lot written about company culture. How to build the best culture, how to fix a broken culture, how to attract the best talent with the best culture and on and on it goes. 

Having spent so much of my life building companies I have tried many different methods of building, shaping and moulding the ideal company culture and there is one thing that consistently leads to the best company culture: Curiosity. 

A culture of curiosity permeates every aspect of your business. Curious people ask more questions and find new and interesting solutions to problems. Curious people engage with one another in a deeper and more meaningful way. Curious people are often more interested in listening than in speaking. Curiosity is also the god particle of innovation. So when other’s are trying to innovate, curious companies are asking more questions and finding better answers. 

Curiosity speaks to a lot of different areas of a business including the kind of person you hire to join your team, the kind of work you allow people to do and the psychological safety of the people in your business.
