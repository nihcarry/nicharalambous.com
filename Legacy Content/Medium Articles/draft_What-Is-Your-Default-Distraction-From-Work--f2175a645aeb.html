What Is Your Default Distraction From Work?

Growing an audience online is no trivial task. 

I have been posting content on Twitter since April 2007. 

  • 15 years of content
  • ±29 600 individual tweets
  • 1973 tweets per year
  • 5.4 tweets per day (including replies) for 15 years

In short, I am committed to Twitter. 

But I’m not sure Twitter or my audience is committed to me. 

After so many years on this platform, I find myself stuck in a rut. My follower count isn’t growing, my audience engagement has bottomed out and I’m not really sure what to do about it. I’m tweeting into the wind but I am also not just tweeting. I also produce a podcast once a week. I send a newsletter once a week. I write a blog post once a week. I post on LinkedIn every single day. I host live streams twice a month answering business questions. I host a private monthly call for entrepreneurs building side hustles. I am active. 

The first thing I know about content right now is that the world rewards polarization and strong opinions.

The second thing I know is that focus matters (you can’t tweet about business, politics, marketing, investing, bird watching, board games, clothing, pet care and everything in between). 

The third thing I thought I knew was that people wanted to engage with content and click on links. 

But then Rich Mulholland and I were talking about this problem and I think we stumbled upon a concept that might help me (and hopefully you, too) unlock more value for my content generally. 

Answer a question for me: When you have 8 minutes between meetings, what do you do? What platform do you turn to? Which app do you open? Said another way: 

What is your Default Distraction?

Do you open up TikTok and swipe up past 30 or 40 fifteen second videos? Do you whip open the Reddit app and find your favourite subreddit and dive deeply into a single post? Do you read an article that you’ve had open for a week in a tab hidden amongst hundreds of opened and unread tabs? Do you scroll through Instagram stories or watch a useful 8 minute YouTube video?

What is your Default Distraction?

Why does this even matter to me and my Twitter problem? Well, mostly because I think I’m fighting for people’s attention, right? Aren’t we all? And if I’m trying to fight for your attention on Twitter with a link to my latest podcast episode that is an hour-long, you’re probably not going to click it and start listening, are you? No. No, you’re not. You’re going to find your default distraction and pop through a few more TikToks/Reels/Stories/Snaps/YT Shorts/etc and then dive into your next meeting. 

Rich did something with his newsletter that I think is smart and could provide an answer to the default distraction issue. For a while he shared his newsletter with his favourite links from the week on a Saturday morning. 

The prevailing knowledge is that if you want to consistently engage an audience then you have to consistently post about a core theme as frequently as you can cope with and build a loyal following of people who read and share everything you do. Consistency is the play and focusing on a single core area of authority is the method.
